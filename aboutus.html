About Rotaract

Rotaract is a global network of young professionals and university students committed to making a positive impact in their communities. As a part of Rotary International, Rotaract provides opportunities for personal growth, leadership development, and community service.

Our members are passionate about creating positive change, fostering international understanding, and developing lifelong friendships. Through local and international service projects, professional networking, and social activities, Rotaract offers a platform for young adults to connect, learn, and take action.

Rotary Areas Of Focus

Here are the Rotary Seven Areas of Focus

The Rotary Foundation, a global organization dedicated to service and humanitarian work, focuses on seven key areas known as the Rotary Seven Areas of Focus. These areas encompass a range of sectors where Rotary members work to make a positive and lasting impact on local and international communities.

Peacebuilding and Conflict Resolution

Rotaract encourages conversations to foster understanding within and across cultures. We train adults and young leaders to prevent and mediate conflict and help refugees who have fled dangerous areas.

Disease Prevention and Treatment

Focusing on initiatives to prevent and control diseases, improve access to healthcare, and support medical research and interventions.

Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene (WASH)

Addressing water scarcity, improving sanitation facilities, and promoting hygiene practices to enhance public health and well-being.

Maternal and Child Health

Supporting programs and services that improve the health and well-being of mothers and children, including prenatal care, nutrition, immunizations, and access to healthcare.

Basic Education and Literacy

Promoting access to quality education, enhancing literacy skills, and supporting educational infrastructure and resources.

Economic and Community Development

Initiating projects and activities that stimulate economic growth, entrepreneurship, and sustainable development within communities, focusing on poverty alleviation and empowerment.

Supporting the Environment

Rotary recognizes the importance of environmental conservation and sustainability. Rotary members engage in activities such as tree planting, environmental education, waste management projects, and efforts to mitigate climate change.

History of Rotaract

Rotaract was founded in 1968 by Rotary International as a Rotary-sponsored program for young adults aged 18 to 30. It was established to address the needs of young professionals and students who wanted to make a positive impact in their communities.

The first Rotaract club was formed at the University of North Carolina in the United States. As word spread about the program's success, Rotaract clubs began to emerge worldwide. Today, Rotaract boasts thousands of clubs in over 180 countries and geographical areas, making it one of the largest global networks of young leaders.

Rotaract clubs are guided by the same core values as Rotary International: service, leadership, diversity, and fellowship. They engage in a wide range of community service projects, including environmental initiatives, educational programs, healthcare campaigns, and poverty alleviation efforts.

Rotaract also offers young adults unique opportunities for personal and professional development. Members have the chance to enhance their leadership skills, expand their networks, and gain valuable experiences that help shape their future careers.

Throughout its history, Rotaract has played a pivotal role in fostering cultural understanding, promoting peace and goodwill, and empowering young leaders to create lasting change. By providing a platform for young adults to connect, learn, and take action, Rotaract continues to inspire and equip the next generation of global citizens.
